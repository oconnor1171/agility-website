Action Plan — Operational Recommendations

Priority Action Items

High Priority
Subcontractor 1099 Documentation Compliance
51 cash-paid subcontractor transactions totaling $127,340 lack W-9 documentation. By IRC §6041, all 1099-eligible payments must be documented. Action: Request outstanding W-9s within 30 days. Implement digital form collection workflow for future contracts. Estimated risk: underreported 1099s could trigger IRS penalties.
High Priority
Supplier Vendor Master Cleanup
67 of 154 supplier transactions ($89,230) lack consistent vendor naming in QuickBooks. This blocks vendor concentration analysis and duplicate invoice detection. Action: Standardize all supplier names using a master vendor list. Enable payment term negotiation and volume reporting. Timeline: 2-3 weeks.
Medium Priority
Fleet Fuel Cost Monitoring
Vehicle fuel costs spiked 18% in February, with coded entries lacking pump details and odometer readings. This suggests tracking gaps. Action: Implement fuel card system with driver login and pump location capture. Establish fuel cost benchmarks per vehicle class. Review for personal-use risk.
Medium Priority
Software & Subscription Audit
Office software spend of $8,400 in Q1 is 30% above benchmark for a 4-FTE shop. Review active licenses, discontinue unused tools, and consolidate overlapping platforms. Potential savings: $2,000–$3,000 annually.
Medium Priority
Payroll Classification and Job Costing
Confirm that field supervisor and foreman time is properly allocated to specific projects for accurate job costing. Current QuickBooks structure allocates payroll to general ledger without project linkage. Implement time-tracking integration to improve project profitability analysis.
Low Priority
Equipment Capitalization Review
12 equipment purchases totaling $18,200 require review for capitalization thresholds and depreciation methods. Ensure compliance with ASC 360 and tax regulations. Recommend annual fixed asset audit and reconciliation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this an audit or a review?

+

No. This is a management-use financial analysis engagement — not an audit, review, or compilation under SSARS/AICPA standards. We prepare financial statements from your data with CPA-level rigor, but we do not express an opinion or provide assurance. If you need attested financials for lending or bonding, we can arrange that under a separate engagement.

Does this include tax advice?

+

This financial analysis product is not tax advice — it's a financial clarity tool. However, Agility Accountants & Advisors absolutely provides tax planning, tax preparation, and strategic tax advisory as separate services. Our financial packages include CPA footnotes with IRC section references that flag tax treatment questions, making your tax engagement more efficient and thorough.

What data do I need to get started?

+

At minimum: 12 months of bank statements and credit card statements (PDF or CSV). That's enough for a full Income Statement and operational benchmarking. If you have accounting software exports, prior tax returns, or payroll summaries, the analysis gets even deeper.

How is this different from my bookkeeper?

+

Your bookkeeper categorizes transactions. We do that — plus we benchmark every expense category against your industry peers, identify where you're overspending, quantify the dollar impact, and give you specific corrective actions. Every benchmark maps directly back to your Income Statement line items, so there's full traceability from the high-level analysis down to individual transactions. Most bookkeepers don't touch operational analysis.

Do you work with my existing CPA?

+

We can do both. If you have a CPA you trust, our deliverables include CPA-ready footnotes with IRC section references — your advisor gets a clean, organized package instead of a shoebox. But we're also a full-service CPA firm offering tax planning, tax preparation, bookkeeping, and advisory services. Many clients find that our financial analysis delivers more detail and visibility than what they're currently getting, and consolidating with one firm eliminates redundant costs.

What industries do you cover?

19 NAICS classifications with industry-specific benchmarks: construction (residential, commercial, specialty trade, heavy highway), restaurants (full service and limited service), IT services, medical/dental, legal, trucking, property management, landscaping, trades, retail, manufacturing, wholesale, and accounting firms.
